Project information

  • Customer: Casa Colorada / Museo de Santiago (Santiago de Chile)
  • Product: Audio guide
  • Content production by Nubart: No
  • Year of launch: 2024
  • Project status: Active


Casa Colorada's audio guide card

This building represents one of the most important preserved examples of the Chilean urban colonial house. It was built in 1769 by commission of Mateo de Toro y Zambrano to the Portuguese architect Joseph de la Vega. Currently, the Casa Colorada houses the exhibition rooms of the Museum of Santiago. After the 2010 earthquake, it suffered significant damage and was closed to undergo a restoration program. In August 2024 it finally reopened its doors to once again share its legacy with the citizens.

Nubart's audio guide card delivers the Casa Colorada's permanent exhibitions in three languages (Spanish, English and Portuguese). The content has been produced by the Customer.
