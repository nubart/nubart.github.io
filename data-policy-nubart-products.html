Policy for audio guides

General privacy rules applies to all Nubart products


This Privacy and Data Protection Policy provides information to the users of our digital products about the policies and procedures as to the nature, scope and purpose of the collection, use and disclosure of personal and other data.

The legal basis for the data protection policy can be found in particular in the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)


Permissions

If you use our web-app to scan one of our QR-codes, the browser will request permission to access your camera. We will use your camera exclusively for the purpose of scanning the QR code. Under no circumstances will we use this access to take other types of images with it, nor will we keep any images that may result from its use.


Access data / Server log files

On our Nubart-products, like audioguides or networking tools, we collect and store some access data from the user, such as country of origin, date and time of access, language setting of the browser used, retrieved files and amount of data transmitted, messages about successful retrievals, browser type and version, the user's operating system. We don't hold or store IP address data.

We only use this information for statistical analysis in order to operate our system, preserve security and improve our Services.

Our database is stored with Amazon Web Services on their database in Ireland (EU). We do not transfer personal data to countries outside the EU that don't provide an adequate level of data protection according to the GDPR.


Cookies

Cookies are small files that store specific device-related information on your access device (PC, smartphone, etc.). We only use cookies as above mentioned, for technical reasons to make access to the audio guide easier and to recognize the browser used in order to save, for example, the language configuration you have chosen. Its use is strictly functional and serves no other purpose.

You can modify your browser's settings to determine the use of cookies, such as to restrict or completely prevent their storage. You can also delete cookies from your browser at any time. However, our audio guides may not function properly and with ease, if you disable cookies.


Information, correction, blocking and deletion

You may receive free information regarding the personal data we have collected about you. In addition, you may correct inaccurate data, and block, delete, or modify your personal data at any time, as long as there are no legal storage requirements or other legal reasons. Please send related requests to info@nubart.eu.




Specific policy for Nubart's audio guides
Personal Information

We only collect and use personal information particular to you, such as your name, e-mail address or phone number, when permitted by law or if you expressly consent to it.

This would be the case, for example, if you contacted us. In that case, in order to process your request, the collection and storage of your personal data is necessary and permitted.

When using our audio guide, initially we only collect and keep the user code that you have received from the museum and that doesn't allow us to infer any of your personal data. A cookie used on your device (see below) will allow you to use our audio guide from the same device at any time. If you want to use our audio guide from a different device or from a device where you have deleted the cookie, you will require a new activation link that you'll receive once you have entered your email address. This email address will be stored by us only to ease future transmissions of the activation link.

We will never share with third parties the personal data collected this way, not even to our client (museum, organizer or venue), unless we are legally bound to do so. We will never use your email address to send you commercial communications from us or from third parties.
