Nubart Heritage

Interactive multimedia audio guides for contemporary history museums


Nubart offers an interactive multimedia guide that is much more than a companion to your exhibition.

Heritage museums often deal with sensitive issues. Nubart will help you to control the message and place the exhibits in their historical context.

Its upload button will also allow your visitors to easily enrich your archive with new documents and testimonials that otherwise would get lost.

With our collapsable "more-info"-button you can complement your tour with in-depth information for your visitors to check at home.

Our cards are non-transferable, but reusable by its legitimate owner. They are hygienic, easy to use and don't have to be collected, since visitors keep them.

Printed on eco-friendly materials and 100% carbon-neutral.


Complement the tour with more information

The mission of your museum is not only to accompany the visitor during the tour. Nubart is an audio guide visitors keep.

Nubart's flexible modular system and its drop-down "more information" interface allows visitors to access hundreds of multimedia materials to enrich their information: videos and audios with testimonials, PDFs with archival documents that can be consulted in the same audio guide or downloaded, image sliders... Whatever and however much you want!

Help your visitors delve deeper at home into everything they have discovered in your museum, without distracting them from the tour.

Enrich your collection

Thousands of historic photographs, personal letters, testimonies and forgotten data rest in drawers and often end up being lost forever. The official donation of archival materials is often a bureaucratic and laborious process. Help your visitors break the ice!

Let your visitors contribute to enriching your museum's collection by providing them with a spontaneous, quick and - if they wish - anonymous way to upload materials and testimonials to your password-protected customer-area. You can review these materials later, and decide what you want to do with them. Some hidden gems may enrich your museum's archive!
