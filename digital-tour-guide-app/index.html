The wireless tour guide system that needs no receivers


Leading factory visits, city tours, or group excursions in noisy or challenging environments? Struggling to keep everyone engaged when half the group can't hear you?

Nubart® LIVE is a smartphone-based tour guide system that replaces traditional receivers and radio guides entirely. The guide speaks normally into their smartphone; participants scan a QR code card and hear crystal-clear audio through their own headphones — even in the loudest environments.

No hardware fleet. No app downloads. No equipment logistics. Just reusable QR code cards and an internet connection.

Why a QR code per participant make tours smoother

Unlike systems where everyone scans a shared QR code or enters a PIN, every Nubart LIVE code is unique and non-transferable.

  Everyone joins instantly: No crowding around a single screen. Each participant scans their own code and is ready to go — all at once.

  Independent and stress-free: If someone disconnects, they simply scan again. No need to ask the guide or interrupt the tour.

  Full control for the guide: Only invited participants can join. No unexpected listeners and no interference between groups.

    During the Tour

    This is not a problem at all.

    When opening a group as a guide, we recommend scanning a few extra cards in case someone arrives late.

    If this happens, simply hand one of those already scanned cards to the late arrival. That’s all — they will be able to join the group immediately.

    Once you close the group, you can reuse those extra cards for the next tour. Cards only become non-transferable once participants scan them themselves, not when the guide scans them.

    A small delay of up to one second between the guide and participants (latency) is unavoidable with internet-based systems.

    Latency is affected by smartphone performance, connection quality, and whether participants use the same mobile data provider.

    However, if the guide speaks in a low voice, the latency is barely noticeable—participants will primarily hear the amplified voice from their smartphone rather than an echo from the guide's natural voice.

    If you want to take the call, just tap "mute" and answer your phone.

    The group will remain connected but on hold while you talk. When you're done with the call, return to the browser. If some participants show a red light instead of green, tap "reload"—everyone will reconnect without needing to rescan their QR codes.

    Note: On Android, if you forget to mute before answering, participants may hear your side of the conversation. On iPhone, they won't hear anything.

    The system will automatically reconnect when the connection is restored. Participants do not need to rescan their cards.

    Participants can use their phone's speaker. However, headphones provide better audio quality and prevent the guide's amplified voice from creating an echo effect.

    AI Translation Add-on

    The guide selects the language they will speak and activates the translator. Participants scan the QR code and choose their preferred language from 35+ options. They can either listen to the guide's original voice or receive real-time AI translation as both text and audio on their screen.

    See our instructions to know more.

    The Nubart LIVE AI-powered translation add-on currently supports Arabic, Bulgarian, Cantonese, Catalan, Chinese, Czech, Danish, Dutch, English, Estonian, Filipino, Finnish, French, German, Greek, Hebrew, Hungarian, Indonesian, Italian, Japanese, Korean, Latvian, Lithuanian, Norwegian, Polish, Portuguese, Romanian, Russian, Slovak, Slovenian, Spanish, Swedish, Thai, Turkish, Ukrainian, and Vietnamese.

    Google Translate is a great personal tool for translating short phrases, signs or simple conversations on your own device. However, it is not designed for guiding a moving group of visitors in real time.

    While Google Translate can play short audio snippets, it is not designed to provide a continuous, hands-free audio stream. Each visitor has to manage the app individually, start and stop the microphone, trigger playback, and read small text on their screen. Timing, interruptions and delays vary from person to person, which quickly becomes confusing in a group setting.

    Nubart LIVE is built specifically for guided tours and factory visits. One guide speaks once into a microphone, and all participants receive the same audio stream in sync on their own smartphones — no app download required.

    With the optional AI translation add-on, the translation is directly linked to the guide’s live audio stream. Participants simply choose their preferred language and follow the tour together, instead of each person running a separate translation app.

    In short: Google Translate is ideal for individual use; Nubart LIVE is designed for shared, synchronized guided experiences.

    Technical Details

    All the "background work" of our technology takes place on the guide's smartphone. It is therefore desirable that your guides have a modern smartphone with a good processor and a reasonably new battery.

    However, this does not affect the group members: for them the battery load is not much higher than when listening to a podcast or an audiobook. For example, one hour of continuous use consumes 8% of the battery on a 4 year old Android phone.

    About 20 MB per hour. Since our system is unidirectional (only the guide speaks) and we do not broadcast video, our system uses much less data than a video call in Zoom or Skype. Even less than a regular phone call!

    Yes — and for many tour operators, this is actually the recommended setup. A portable WiFi hotspot with a local prepaid SIM card is all you need. The guide carries the hotspot, participants connect to it as they would any WiFi network, and turn off data roaming on their own phones. They incur no data charges whatsoever.

    Nubart LIVE works with any standard hotspot device and any SIM card — you are free to choose whatever hardware and data plan best suits your budget and destination. This approach is especially useful when your group includes international visitors who may face roaming charges, when tours take place in areas with patchy mobile coverage, or simply when you want to guarantee a consistent connection regardless of each participant's phone plan or carrier.

    Yes, significantly. Without translation, Nubart LIVE uses only about 20 MB per hour regardless of group size — less than a regular phone call. With the AI translation add-on active, each participant receives their own individually translated audio stream, so consumption scales with the number of listeners. For a group of 50 people over a full day of guiding, expect roughly 10–11 GB in total.

    If you are running multi-day tours or large groups with translation active, we recommend sizing your data plan accordingly — or simply choosing an unlimited option, which typically costs only a few euros more and removes any concern about running out mid-tour.

    For WiFi-only use, your network should support the number of concurrent users expected and allow peer-to-peer connections (WebRTC). Contact us for specific technical requirements.

    Each Nubart LIVE card contains a unique, non-transferable code. This is a deliberate design choice that solves real problems other systems ignore.

    No bottleneck at tour start: Instead of 25 people queuing to scan one small phone screen, each participant scans their own card independently. The guide can even pre-scan the cards before handing them out, forming the group in advance.

    Session recovery: Tourists constantly leave their browser to take photos, check messages, or use other apps. With a shared digital code, they lose their connection and have to interrupt the guide. With a Nubart LIVE card, they simply re-scan and rejoin instantly — no guide assistance needed.

    Access control: Unlike systems where anyone who knows a PIN or sees a QR code can join, Nubart LIVE gives the guide full control over who is in the group. No uninvited listeners, no confusion between overlapping tour groups at the same location.
