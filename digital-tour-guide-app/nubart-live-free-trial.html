How to Test Nubart LIVE

Web-based group guide system — trial instructions

Welcome to your Nubart LIVE trial! Your trial includes 30 minutes of free AI simultaneous translation — entirely optional, the system works perfectly well without it.

We recommend reading through these steps once before you start. The whole process is straightforward, and a quick read will make the test much smoother.

Before you meet your group — do these two steps first
1

Register as the guide and create your password

Click the registration link above. You will be asked to choose a password and log in.

Once logged in, you will see the guide interface. You can close it afterwards — you are all set for the test.

Tip for the day of the test: Bookmark www.nub.art/customer/login on your smartphone now, so you can log in quickly when you are with your group.
2

Print and cut the 6 participant cards

Print this document and cut out the QR cards on the next page. Keep them ready to hand out when you meet your group.

Each code is unique and non-transferable. One card per person — do not share or swap them.
These printed cards are for testing purposes only. If you decide to go ahead with Nubart LIVE, your participants will receive proper cards — printed on FSC-certified high-quality cardboard (customised with your branding if you order 3,000 cards or more).
Running the test — do these steps with your group
3

Log in and open the group

On your smartphone, go to www.nub.art/customer/login (or use your bookmark) and log in with the password you created in Step 1.

Once logged in, tap "Open group".

AI translation: right after logging in, a pop-up will ask whether you want to activate the translation add-on. You can enable or skip it here — you can always change this later.
4

Scan the participant cards with your phone

Tap "Open scanner" on the guide screen and scan the QR cards of the participants joining your test — you do not need to scan all 6 if you are only testing with a smaller group.

We recommend scanning the cards before handing them out — it is the easiest approach. That said, participants can also scan their own card first and then show it to you. Both work fine.
5

Hand out the cards — ask participants to scan their own

Give one card per person. Each participant scans the QR code on their own card with their smartphone.

Tap "View participants" on your guide screen. The green indicator next to each slot will light up as each participant completes the connection from their own device.

6

Start talking — and try the AI translation if you wish

Tap "Unmute" and speak. You will see a moving bar on screen — this confirms your voice is being transmitted to all participants.

If translation is active: participants receive it automatically in their browser language. Important for testing: if your colleagues' phones have the same browser language as yours, they will hear the original voice rather than a translation. Ask them to tap the (translate) icon on their screen to manually select a different language.
After the test
7

Keep the group open or close it

If you want to run another test with the same group later, simply leave the group open. Next time you log in, your participants will already be connected — no need to scan the cards again.

If you are done testing, tap "Close group" to end the session.

What participants will see and can do:
Once connected, participants hear the guide through their own smartphone. If translation is active, they receive it automatically in their browser language — or they can tap the icon to pick a different one. They can also send a written question to the guide at any time without interrupting — the guide sees these in a list and can reply when convenient.
Troubleshooting
You are unmuted but participants hear nothing

Check that the moving bar (spectrum display) is reacting when you speak. If it is not:

  • Another app may be using your microphone — close all background apps and try again
  • If you are using a Bluetooth or wireless microphone, switch to Firefox browser, which handles external audio devices more reliably than Chrome or Safari
Participants see the screen but hear no sound

Ask them to:

  • Turn up their phone volume
  • Check that the audio is not muted in the participant interface
  • Close any other app that might be using sound (music players, videos, etc.)
The translation does not make sense

This is almost always caused by poor audio input. Check the audio quality indicator on your guide screen:

  • Green = audio is clear enough for translation
  • Yellow or red = audio quality needs improvement

To improve quality: use a headset with a microphone close to your mouth to isolate your voice from the environment, speak clearly, and confirm the correct speaking language is selected.

The translation keeps repeating sentences

Your microphone is probably picking up the translated audio output from nearby devices. Try:

  • Using a headset with a microphone
  • Asking participants to use headphones
  • Increasing the distance between phones
  • Lowering the volume on nearby devices
A participant does not appear in the participants list

They may not have scanned their card yet. Ask them to scan their own QR card again — or, if they cannot scan, to go to nub.art and type in the private code printed on their card. The connection can only be established from the participant's own device.

The connection drops during the test

This is usually a temporary coverage gap. Simply move a few metres with your group — the connection will recover automatically once coverage is available again.
