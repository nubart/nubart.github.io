How to get the most out of Nubart LIVE for your group travels

Organizing a group trip can present communication challenges for the guide or teacher, especially in noisy environments. Nubart LIVE offers a simple and cost-effective solution for guides, teachers and organizers, allowing all participants to clearly hear the explanations through their own cell phones. In this article we share useful recommendations to prepare and make the most of Nubart LIVE in your next field trips.

Why it makes sense to have a group guidance system in your travels

Urban environments can be very noisy and, without the right technical tools to get his voice across to all members of the group, the guide is forced to shout and strain his voice. In addition, in some very touristic cities or cities with narrow streets, the use of technical group guiding systems is mandatory to reduce the acoustic disturbance that tour groups cause to the inhabitants. Guided tour systems are also mandatory in many museums.

However, traditional tour guide equipment is expensive, heavy and requires cleaning and maintenance. Normally only professional tour guides are motivated enough to invest in such equipment.

But amateur guides who organize occasional guided tours or docents who organize a field trip with their students need a solution like Nubart LIVE.

Nubart LIVE is much more economical than traditional radio guides, easy to use and maintenance-free, as both the guide and the participants use their own smartphones.

Here are a few things to keep in mind to make your trip with Nubart LIVE a real success:


Test the system before your trip.

Nubart will be happy to send you a free trial kit, which you can order here. Cut out and distribute the codes we will send you and do a pre-test in conditions similar to your trip to familiarize yourself with the system. As far as possible, use the same smartphone for the test that you will be using on your trip. This way, if necessary, you will have time to give permissions for the navigator to access the microphone, for example.


Make sure there is good mobile coverage in the city of your travel destination.

Unlike traditional radio guides, Nubart LIVE transmits the voice signal over the Internet. It works perfectly with wifi, but on an outdoor trip it will be unavoidable to use mobile data. Keep in mind that good internet coverage is not available everywhere. It may be a good idea to check mobile network coverage before your trip. There are several free services that show coverage maps for local mobile network operators, such as nPerfa:

In case you pass by a place without coverage, simply move away with your group and be patient, as the Nubart LIVE connection will automatically recover as soon as the internet signal is received again.


Use a modern smartphone with a good processor for the guide.

Don't worry about the participants in the group: even very old smartphones work perfectly well in the role of receivers. However, the guide's smartphone acting as a transmitter is where the most technologically challenging process takes place. If you use a very old smartphone as a transmitter, the device can heat up and the battery will quickly run out. For this reason, we recommend using a modern smartphone with a good processor to perform as a guide.


If the trip lasts several days, do not close the session and invite your group to write their name on the card.

Nubart cards are non-transferable, but reusable. If your trip lasts several days, you do not need to hand out a new card each time. However, it is important that each member of the group always uses the same card. A simple trick to avoid confusion is to ask each traveler to write his or her name on his or her card and carry it with them at all times.

We also recommend that you leave the session open on the guide's smartphone. This will save you the trouble of re-scanning all the QR codes the next day.


If there is more than one guide on your trip, share the smartphone or use a dual microphone.

Sometimes you have more than one guide to lead the same group. In Nubart LIVE it is not possible for two guides to use their smartphones simultaneously in the same session. But there are other ways to solve this situation:

  • Guides can pass the transmitting smartphone from hand to hand.
  • A wired USB microphone can be connected to the smartphone and the microphone can be passed from hand to hand
  • A two-piece wireless lavalier USB microphone can be purchased for about 15-25 dollars. With this type of microphones two guides can talk comfortably and be up to 20 meters away from the transmitting phone. Remember to test them before starting your trip!

If the guiding sessions are very long, take a charger or a portable battery with you.

When traveling, you need to use your phone continuously and the battery often gets discharged more than usual. If you are going to use Nubart LIVE for many hours at a time, we recommend that you bring a powerbank that you have pre-charged at your hotel or at least your cell phone charger so that you can recharge your device at a bar or restaurant socket when you take a break.

Also follow these tips to save battery life:

  • The operation of Nubart LIVE prevents screen locking and the screens on consumes battery, so remember to lock the screen of your phone when you are not using it. Also remind your group of this recommendation when you stop talking.
  • During the operation of Nubart LIVE it is necessary to keep the screens unlocked. However, you can significantly reduce the battery consumption during use by reducing the screen brightness as much as possible.

If you keep these details in mind, your experience with Nubart LIVE is sure to be a positive one. Have a good trip!
