How to get the best out of your trial

Test with another person. Testing alone is possible but gives you a much less realistic sense of the experience.
You will need two devices. One acts as the Speaker device (this can be a smartphone or a laptop) and logs in to transmit. The other acts as the Listener device and scans the QR code to receive the translation.
Keep the two devices apart — or use headphones. The AI captures all audio it hears, including the translated audio coming from the listener device. If both devices are next to each other without headphones, the speaker device's microphone will pick up the translation and start translating the translation — creating an echo loop of increasingly inaccurate results.
The listener defaults to the browser's language. If the speaker and listener are both using browsers set to the same language —a very common scenario in a trial!— the listener device will simply repeat the speaker's words in an AI voice, rather than translating them. Ask the listener to manually select a different language using the language selector button on their screen. This will not happen in a real case scenario with foreign listeners.
1

Set Up the Speaker Device

On the Speaker device, go to www.nub.art/customer/login and log in. If this is your first time, use the registration link in the box above to create your account first.

  1. Click Nubart TRANSLATE.
  2. Select the language you will be speaking (not the language you want to translate into).
  3. Tap Unmute and start speaking naturally.
If you change language during the test, always update the selected speaker language before continuing. Forgetting to do this is the most common cause of poor translation quality.
2

Set Up the Listener Device

On the Listener device, scan the QR code at the top of this page (or tap the link below it).

  1. Select the language you want to hear the translation in.
  2. Make sure your volume is turned up.
  3. No login, no app, no permissions required.
For the clearest audio experience, use headphones or earphones on the listener device.
3

Check Translation Quality

On the speaker interface, you will see two quality indicators:

  • Spectrum display: the bars should move when you speak. If they don't move, your microphone is not active or the wrong input is selected.
  • Audio quality indicator: aim for green (80%+). Yellow is usable; red means the translation quality will be poor and you should check your audio setup.
