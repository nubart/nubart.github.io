Phase 1 of 2 — GPS & Connectivity Validation This demo is the first of two steps towards your final audio guide setup. Its purpose is twofold: for you, to experience how tracks trigger automatically as your vehicle passes each point of interest; for us, to review the geo-tracking heatmaps generated during your route and assess internet and GPS coverage along the way. The results of this phase will give both of us the information we need to move forward confidently with Phase 2, where we configure the full remote control system for your passengers. Please note any tracks that trigger too early, too late, or not at all — everything can be fine-tuned once we have reviewed the data together.
1

Before You Start: Prepare Your Device

Grant geolocation permissions. Because Nubart is web-based (not a native app), your browser needs location access. You will be prompted automatically when you scan the QR code — tap Allow for all requests. Do not manually lock your screen during the test.

Set your volume high enough before you begin.

If the demo does not trigger correctly, the issue is usually related to location permissions. These can exist at three levels:

  • System level — your phone's operating system settings
  • Browser level — find your browser under "Applications" in system settings and enable location access there
  • Domain level — our guides run on nub.art. In your browser's Privacy & Security settings, confirm that nub.art has location access enabled.

If your demo is in offline mode, wait until all tracks have finished preloading before moving on.

2

Check the First Track — at Your Office

We have placed a temporary track at your office location. Scan the QR code above and wait a moment. If the track starts playing automatically, your permissions are correctly set and you are ready to go out on the route.

If the track plays at your office, everything is working — head straight to the vehicle.

If nothing plays, check the Troubleshooting section below before proceeding.

3

On the Tour

Take your smartphone on your vehicle and run your route as normal. Tracks will play automatically as you approach each point of interest.

  • Make sure mobile data is enabled during the entire route.
  • Keep the browser open and the screen active — do not lock it manually.
  • Hold the phone close to a window if you notice triggering issues.
  • If a track plays slightly early or late, note it — triggering zones can be adjusted.
  • To repeat the test, refresh the page or re-scan the QR code first so the guide restarts from the beginning.
We have geo-tracking enabled for your demo. After your test we will review the heatmaps to identify any connectivity gaps and discuss solutions with you. Your Nubart contact person will review the results with you.
Troubleshooting
GPS works on Chrome (Android) but not on Safari (iPhone)

Safari on iPhone requires geolocation to be enabled at three separate levels. Please check each one:

  1. System level: Go to Settings → Privacy & Security → Location Services and make sure Location Services is switched on.
  2. Browser level: Still in Settings → Privacy & Security → Location Services, scroll down to find Safari and set it to While Using the App.
  3. Domain level: Open Safari, go to Settings → Apps → Safari → Settings for Websites → Location and make sure it is not set to Deny.

Also make sure you have selected Precise Location rather than a general area. Once all three levels are enabled, reload the page and try again.

Track plays but there is no sound

Another app may be capturing your audio output. Close all background apps and try again. Restart your phone if necessary.

Track is not triggered at your location

Open the map in the audio guide and tap the small figure icon in the upper left corner. The map should centre on your current position. If it does not, check all three permission levels described in Step 1.

Location visible but jumps around on the map

Make sure you have granted precise location access rather than just a general area. If jumps persist outdoors, your device may have a weak GPS chipset — try with a different smartphone model.

A few tracks do not trigger at all

These points likely fall in areas with limited internet or GPS coverage. Note which ones and let us know — in rare coverage gaps, we can apply alternative triggering strategies, like timed triggers.

Many tracks are not triggering

This usually means there is no reliable internet or GPS signal in your vehicle. Try refreshing and repeating the route with the phone held close to a window. If the problem persists across the whole route, your area may need a dedicated antenna — contact us and we will advise.
