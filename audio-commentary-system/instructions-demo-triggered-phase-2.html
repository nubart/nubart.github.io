Phase 2 of 2 — Remote Control Validation Phase 1 confirmed that GPS and connectivity work along your route. Now we test the production setup: a dedicated master smartphone on board your vehicle controls all audio triggering centrally, while passengers simply scan a QR code and listen in their chosen language — no permissions, no interaction required on their part. This is the experience your passengers will have from day one.

Why remote control instead of individual GPS?

  • No permissions needed from passengers. GPS on smartphones requires browser location permissions, which are confusing — especially on iPhones. With a master smartphone, passengers don't need to grant anything.
  • Works even when passengers switch apps. Passengers may take photos, check messages, or briefly lock their screen. Tracks still trigger correctly via the master smartphone regardless.
  • More consistent GPS accuracy. A single, well-positioned master device with a good GPS chipset is far more reliable than depending on dozens of different passenger phones.
You will need two smartphones for this test.
Master smartphone The device that controls triggering. Log in here and keep the Remote Control screen open. Use a phone with a good GPS chipset (e.g. Samsung Galaxy S23) and place it at the highest point of the vehicle, near a window. Keep it connected to power and never lock the screen.

Passenger device Any second smartphone. Scan the QR code above and select a language. This simulates what your passengers will experience.

Recommended Equipment & Browsers

GPS accuracy depends heavily on the device used. For the master smartphone, use a model with a reliable GPS chipset (e.g. Samsung Galaxy S23 or equivalent). Avoid budget models or older iPhones for this role.

  • Master smartphone (Android): use Chrome
  • Master smartphone (iPhone): use Safari
  • Passenger device: any browser will work — passengers do not use GPS
Do not use Firefox on the master smartphone. Firefox does not have full access to Google's or Apple's location databases, which can cause tracks to trigger too early, too late, or not at all.

Log In on the Master Smartphone

On the Master smartphone, open the login link shown at the top of this page. If this is your first time, use the registration link to set your password first, then log in.

Once logged in:

  1. Tap Remote Control in the menu.
  2. Select your tour from the list.
  3. Keep this screen open for the entire test — do not navigate away or lock the screen.
The Remote Control screen must remain visible on the master smartphone throughout the journey. If the screen locks or the browser is closed, triggering will stop.
3a

Testing Manual Remote Control

If your demo is configured for manual remote control, you trigger each track yourself from the master smartphone.

  1. On the Master smartphone, tap any track name in the Remote Control screen.
  2. On the Passenger device, that track should start playing automatically in the selected language.
  3. Tracks that have already been sent will change colour on the master smartphone screen.
To repeat the test with colours reset, tap Restart Tour at the bottom of the Remote Control screen. This is optional — the remote control works regardless, but resetting lets you see the original track colours again.
3b

Testing GPS-Triggered Remote Control

If your demo is configured for GPS-triggered remote control, no manual action is needed. The master smartphone reads its own GPS position and triggers tracks automatically when the vehicle crosses the predefined points.

  1. Place the Master smartphone at the highest point of the vehicle, near a window, connected to power.
  2. Keep the Remote Control screen open and the screen unlocked.
  3. Run your route. Tracks should play automatically on the Passenger device at the right moments.
  4. On the master smartphone, tracks change colour once they have been sent.
If a track plays slightly early or late, note it. Triggering zones can be adjusted after the test.
To repeat the test with colours reset, tap Restart Tour at the bottom of the Remote Control screen.
Troubleshooting
Passenger device receives no audio at all

Make sure the Remote Control screen is still open on the master smartphone and the screen has not locked. Check that the passenger device has its volume turned up and no background app is capturing the audio output.

GPS-triggered tracks are not firing (master smartphone)

Check that the master smartphone has location permissions enabled in both the system settings and the browser settings. Make sure you are using Chrome (Android) or Safari (iPhone) — not Firefox. Try holding the phone closer to a window or moving to a higher position on the vehicle.

GPS works on Chrome but not on Safari (iPhone master smartphone)

Safari on iPhone requires geolocation enabled at three levels:

  1. System: Settings → Privacy & Security → Location Services → ON
  2. Browser: same menu → find Safari → set to While Using the App
  3. Domain: Settings → Apps → Safari → Settings for Websites → Location → not Deny. Or tap AA in Safari's address bar → Website Settings → Location → Allow.

Also confirm Precise Location is enabled, then reload the page.

Location jumps around on the master smartphone

Make sure precise location is granted, not just a general area. If the problem persists outdoors, the device may have a weak GPS chipset — try a different smartphone model.

Remote Control screen disappeared / session expired

Log back in at www.nub.art/customer/login, go to Remote Control, and select your tour again. To avoid this, keep the screen on and the browser open at all times.
