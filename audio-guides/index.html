A flexible digital audio guide system


Nubart GUIDE is a progressive web-app (PWA) that is accessed via QR-code. It delivers engaging, self-guided museum tours: multilingual, multimedia, and accessible, with nothing for visitors to download or carry.

Choose the delivery method that fits your institution: unique, non-transferable QR cards for monetization, digital codes for integration with online ticketing, or a generic QR code for open and free access.

All options offer a smooth user experience directly on any smartphone, with rich content and effortless navigation.

Our guides are accessible and GDPR-compliant by design.

Museums and historical sites of any kind benefit from powerful built-in visitor feedback and usage insights, privacy-respecting and actionable.

An audio guide is an important additional service offered by your museum. However, only 15-30% of your visitors will want to use it. The production of an audio tour is expensive, so you might want to try to get some of that cost back. Or you might see your audio guides as a revenue stream for your institution.

If you make the same QR code available to everyone, you won't be able to monetize the audio guide because those links are public and can be shared.

At Nubart, we've patented a system called Lightweight Web Access Control. It lets us recognize the user's device anonymously. The legitimate owner of the audio guide card can access the content again whenever they want, but they can't share it with others. This makes your content more valuable, whether you decide to sell the cards separately or include them in the admission fee.

Although the QR-codes on Nubart's cards are non-transferable, they can be reused by their rightful owners at any time. This is important because being able to keep the card and reuse the audio guide is a big selling point for visitors and increases the take-up rate.

If you wish to waive the cards, we can provide the links or QRs online through our API. This way you can integrate them into your ticketing system.

How does it work?

Our QR codes are non-transferable...

  • Non-transferable: The first device can access the audio guide directly by scanning the QR code.
    The QR code is unique and cannot be shared publicly.
    Most visitors do not go any further.

...but reusable

  • Reusable: Visitors who wish to access the content again from a second device will require an access link via email. Once the link has been clicked, the second device will then inherit the access rights. The link can't be shared.
  • Visitors can still access the content as often as needed from any other device. The access link will always be sent to the previously provided email address. This email address cannot be changed.

Visitors only need internet once -at the start of their visit (e.g., entrance hall with Wi-Fi or mobile signal)- to scan the QR code and preload the complete audio guide (including images). Scanning a QR code is now extremely simple: just open the camera, point, and tap. Since the pandemic, smartphone use among seniors has soared, as many adopted video calls to stay connected. QR codes also became a standard way to access digital menus and information, making everyone familiar with them. Before the pandemic, only 35% of Nubart users knew how to scan a QR code; now it's nearly 99%, even among older adults. Adoption keeps growing every year.
