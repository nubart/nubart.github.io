How to obtain statistical data on visitors to a museum



The importance of audience analysis in museums and cultural centers is undeniable. However, in order to carry out this analysis effectively, it is essential to collect reliable statistical data in sufficient quantity to serve as the basis for such analysis.

But obtaining data from an anonymous audience is no simple task.

Years ago it was common for an airline stewardess to greet each passenger with a hand mysteriously hidden behind her back. This was not a simple courtesy, but a discreet method of collecting data. In reality, the stewardess was hiding a manual passenger counter like this one:


Especially in our digital age, all sorts of more or less ingenious tricks have long been applied to collect information. Some are ethically questionable and their bad reputation has overshadowed other legitimate and privacy-friendly ways.


The difficulty of obtaining data on visitors to a museum

In a museum, the number of visitors is perhaps the easiest figure to obtain: it is enough to count the number of tickets sold.

But from there the situation starts to get complicated.

It is not uncommon for museum reception staff to ask for country of origin or postal code when selling tickets to visitors. But in addition to being a potential nuisance for visitors, the question is also an added complication for staff, especially at busy times.

Moreover, this method does not allow to capture some of the most important data for a museum director: from which country does the visitor come? How long has he/she stayed in the facilities? What did he/she think of the exhibition? Which works interested him/her the most?


Audioguide as a tool for collecting data from museum visitors

A digital audio guide is an ingenious, non-invasive way to obtain data while providing an excellent service to museum visitors.

Nubart audio guides are, technically speaking, a PWA or web application. That means that the visitor uses their mobile browser to access the audio guide content. The browser allows accessing and capturing valuable anonymous data in an automated way, without distracting the staff or disturbing the visitor. And it also does so while protecting privacy, since without a registration or login, it is impossible to access the user's identity, email accounts or phone number. (Obviously, accessing Nubart audioguides does not require registration).

Browser information makes it possible to find out, for example, the visitor's country of origin and usual language, which is of fundamental importance for a museum's marketing approach.

All data collected by Nubart is anonymous and aggregated. In addition, the statistics dashboard has been developed internally. Nubart audioguides do not use Google Analytics or any other third party statistical information service. This aspect is very important to guarantee the privacy of visitors. By not applying third party cookies for data collection, we do not contribute to the large technology companies knowing, identifying and benefiting from this information.

Some traditional audio guides (i.e., devices) also allow you to capture usage data, but never the country of origin.

The statistical reports we provide to the museum or exhibition center also incorporate some data that require complex algorithms to obtain, such as the total time of audio use by each user or visitor or the approximate time spent in the facilities.



Nubart's statistical data dashboard is interactive. It allows you to click and unclick values and zoom in and out, among many other functions.

Nubart is continuously working to improve and complete the graphs it offers. Currently, the graphs and tables offered provide information on:

  • Number of visitors using the audio guide
  • Country of origin
  • Audio guide activity by country
  • Visitor's native language
  • Audioguide activity by language
  • Number of users per day
  • Audio guide activity per day
  • Number of users per hour of the day
  • Audioguide activity by time of day
  • Devices used by users (operating system and browser)
  • Time of use of the audioguide in the museum (interval between the first and last click)
  • Tracks listened to from the audio guide

How to know the opinion of visitors to a museum

So far we have referred to statistical data that can be collected automatically through the browser used to access the audio guide. However, there are some data, such as age or opinion about the exhibition, that can only be obtained by asking the visitor.

The usual methods of obtaining feedback from museum visitors are not satisfactory:

  • Personal surveys: Whether through an expensive agency or with the help of their own staff, some museums place someone with a notepad at the exit to interview visitors who have just seen the exhibit. But visitors have spent several hours on their feet and are tired and impatient. In addition, many will not like to express criticism in the presence of a museum representative.

  • Feedback terminals: The automated variant of personal surveys are the so-called feedback terminals, hot-survey devices common in airports and other public places. Some have a keypad that allows detailed answers to many questions to be recorded. Others display only three emoticons that the visitor must tap as he passes: "Like", "Neutral" and "Dislike". A visitor tired of standing for many hours will hardly want to use the first variant. And the second one is too simple to convey truly relevant information for a museum.

    In addition, the rental and maintenance of these terminals generate a recurring cost of between 70 and 300 euros per month.



Nubart audio guides incorporate a feedback form at the end of the audio guide. This allows to capture the visitor's attention in one of the key moments of a museum visit, which is the end of the tour. Here the structure of Nubart audioguides is particularly advantageous, as they work as a timeline through which the visitor scrolls, virtually reproducing the route of the physical space.

At the end of the Nubart audio guide, the visitor has already finished his visit. He or she has already been able to form an overall impression of the exhibition and the emotion it has caused, be it enthusiasm or disappointment, is still vivid.

The visitor sees the feedback form already opened on his cell phone, without being forwarded to any external page. They can answer it conveniently in a few seconds, either when leaving the exhibition, on the way back to the hotel or in the museum cafeteria. And since the questionnaire is anonymous and non-face-to-face, you will not feel intimidated by the presence of an interviewer and will be able to express yourself freely. This probably explains the high response rate of around 10% of Nubart audio guide users.

The questions in the Nubart feedback questionnaire are customizable. We offer the option to send these comments by email to the museum staff in real time. In any case, all responses are recorded in the customer area.


Data quality is equally important

Nubart cards are provided with a unique and identifiable, yet anonymous code and are handed out to visitors on site. This guarantees the quality and informative value of the data. As they are non-transferable codes or links, they cannot be published on a website or on social networks, which would significantly distort the recorded data. The statistics of the Nubart audioguide cards only apply to visitors who have actually visited the museum.

As part of its services, Nubart offers both the data dashboard and the feedback form free of charge to all its customers.
